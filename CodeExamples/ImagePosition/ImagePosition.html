The position attribute can be applied to any element and is one of the most basic and fundamental attributes. Its options static, relative, absolute, fixed and sticky combined with left, right, top, bottom so set the distances can by used to complete customized an elements position within a website.

The default value is position: static;, which tells the element to follow the normal flow of the website. It will ignore any distance given:

position: relative; allows to offset following image with a given distance top:50px; left:50px; to its supposed position:



position: absolute; removes the element from the normal website-flow and orients itself with the given distance from the borders of the website. The following image has the css properties position: absolute; right: 250px; top: 1100px; Resize your window to see why absolute position might not be the optimal option.

position: sticky; makes the image fixed to your sreen. It will be on the same position on your screen, but only as long as it does not leave Its parents space.

position: fixed; makes the image fixed to your window, meaning it will stay fixed on your screen even though you are scrolling up and down. Useful for having a fixed header.
